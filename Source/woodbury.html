The CCIM The Business Excellence Awards Hall of Fame - F.O. Woodbury


F. Orin Woodbury's professional endeavors transformed the real estate industry, both through the numerous projects he developed as well as his contributions to national real estate organizations. He shaped the real estate infrastructure of Utah through projects such as the Utah State Penitentiary, the Standard Oil refinery and pipeline, and the Remington Arms Plant. He also assisted major industrial and financial companies manage their real estate assets and develop footholds in the West. During the Great Depression he managed Zion's Bank's troubled real estate assets and converted them into successful properties. He also helped companies such as General Motors, Ford Motor Company, and General Electric assemble land for factories and plants.

Mr. Woodbury's organizational and leadership skills helped him create some of today's most established real estate organizations. Locally he served as president of the Salt Lake and Utah State Board of Realtors and was Salesman of the Year. He helped found the Institute of Real Estate Management, and was its national president. He also contributed in creating the Counselors of Real Estate organization and was its national president, in addition to being the first Utahn to receive its CRE designation.

His efforts in all these endeavors were guided by a core set of values including integrity, hard work and the value of a good name. He instilled these values in his children and grandchildren, many of whom joined him in the real estate business he established, Woodbury Corporation. Woodbury Corporation continues to be one of the most respected real estate development firms in the Intermountain West, as its employees strive to uphold the legacy F. Orin Woodbury left behind.
